Targeted Termite Treatment

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan to effectively eliminate the termites from your property. We utilize a variety of proven treatment methods, including liquid termiticides, baiting systems, and direct wood treatments. The specific treatment chosen will depend on the termite species, the severity of the infestation, and the construction of your home.

Liquid Termiticide Applications:

Liquid termiticides are applied to the soil around the perimeter of your home, creating a protective barrier that termites cannot penetrate. As termites attempt to cross the treated area, they are exposed to the termiticide, which is then transferred back to the colony, leading to its eradication. Modern liquid termiticides are designed to be non-repellent, meaning termites do not detect them and will continue to forage through the treated zone.

Termite Baiting Systems:

Baiting systems involve placing bait stations around your property that contain cellulose (a food source for termites) infused with a slow-acting termiticide. Termites feed on the bait and carry it back to their colony, sharing it with other termites. This process gradually eliminates the entire colony. Baiting systems are an effective long-term solution that provides ongoing monitoring and control.

Direct Wood Treatment:

For localized termite infestations within wood structures, we may utilize direct wood treatments. These treatments involve applying termiticides directly to infested wood, eliminating the termites present and preventing future activity. This method is particularly effective for treating areas of active infestation identified during the inspection.

Our technicians are highly skilled in the application of all treatment methods, ensuring they are applied safely and effectively. We follow all industry best practices and regulations to minimize any potential impact on the environment and non-target organisms. Our goal is to eliminate the termite infestation while ensuring the safety of your family and pets.

Preventative Pest Education - Recognizing Termite Problems

Promptly identifying the signs of a termite infestation is essential for minimizing damage and preventing costly repairs. Termites often work undetected, but there are several indicators that can signal their presence. Understanding these signs can help you take action quickly and protect your property.

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

Mud tubes are one of the most common and easily recognizable signs of subterranean termites. These pencil-sized tunnels are made by termites using soil, wood particles, and their saliva. Termites use these tubes as protected pathways to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood). Finding mud tubes on the exterior or interior walls of your foundation, or even extending from the ground to wooden structures, is a strong indication of active termite activity. We carefully inspect for these tubes during our inspections and can quickly identify if they are active or not. Active tubes will often feel moist and fresh, while abandoned tubes will be dry and brittle. It's important not to disturb the tubes yourself, as this can sometimes cause termites to abandon that specific pathway and create new ones elsewhere, making it harder to locate the main nest. Our technicians can assess the mud tubes and determine the best course of action to eliminate the termites using them.

Damaged Wood (Hollow Sound)

Termites consume cellulose, the main component of wood, from the inside out. This means that the exterior surface of infested wood may appear perfectly normal, while the interior is being eaten away. If you tap on wood that has been damaged by termites, it may sound hollow. Also, look for blistering or darkening of the wood. As termites tunnel through the wood, they create galleries and voids, significantly weakening the structural integrity. This damage is often hidden behind walls, under floors, or within structural beams, making it difficult to detect without a professional inspection. Our experienced technicians can identify areas of damaged wood and use specialized tools like moisture meters and sounding devices to assess the extent of the infestation without causing further harm. Identifying areas of damaged wood is crucial for targeting treatment and ensuring all active termite colonies are addressed.

Discarded Wings or Swarmers

Swarming is a natural part of the termite lifecycle, where reproductive termites (swarmers) leave their existing colony to establish new ones. These swarmers are often mistaken for flying ants, but termites have straight antennae and equal-sized wings, while ants have elbowed antennae and unequal-sized wings. Finding discarded termite wings near windowsills, doorways, or light sources, particularly in the spring or fall, is a significant indicator of a nearby termite colony. Swarming typically occurs when conditions are warm and humid. While swarmers themselves do not cause damage, their presence signals that a mature termite colony is present on or around your property and is actively reproducing. Ignoring swarmers means ignoring the source of the problem, which is the established colony causing the damage. Our team can identify the presence of swarmers or discarded wings and quickly locate the source colony to implement effective treatment.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not live in the soil. They tunnel within wooden structures and expel their fecal pellets, known as frass, out of small kick-out holes in the wood. These pellets are typically small, granular, and can accumulate in small piles below infested wood. The appearance of frass can vary depending on the type of wood being consumed. Finding small piles of what looks like sawdust or pepper near wooden furniture, doorframes, or trim could be a sign of drywood termite activity. Identifying the type of termite infestation (subterranean or drywood) is crucial for selecting the appropriate treatment method, as their behaviors and habitats are different. Our technicians are trained to identify different types of termite droppings and distinguish them from other types of pest debris.

Visible Termite Activity

In some cases, you may be able to see live termites. Subterranean termites are often found in damaged wood or while disturbing mud tubes. Drywood termites can sometimes be seen within the tunnels they create in wood. Seeing live termites is a clear sign that you have an active infestation and require immediate professional assistance. Don't attempt to treat visible termites yourself, as this may only temporarily deter them and not eliminate the entire colony. Our team can quickly assess the situation and implement targeted treatments to eliminate the active termites and their colony. Observing termites directly allows us to confirm the species and extent of the infestation more accurately, leading to a more effective treatment plan.

Clicking Sounds in Walls

In some instances, you might hear faint clicking or rustling sounds coming from within your walls. This can be caused by termites feeding on the wood or communicating within their tunnels. While this is a less common sign and can be attributed to other factors, it's worth investigating if combined with other potential indicators of termite activity. Some people describe the sound as a quiet chewing or a very faint tapping. If you notice unusual sounds coming from your walls, especially in conjunction with other potential termite signs, it's always a good idea to have a professional inspection. Our experienced technicians can often identify the source of sounds and determine if they are related to termite activity or other pests.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Severe termite infestations can compromise the structural integrity of your home, leading to sagging floors or ceilings. If you notice any changes in the levelness of your floors or the appearance of your ceilings, it could be a sign of significant termite damage. This level of damage indicates a long-standing infestation that has significantly weakened supportive wooden structures. Addressing such issues requires both professional pest control and potentially structural repairs. Detecting these advanced signs emphasizes the importance of early detection and intervention to prevent such extensive and costly damage. Our team can assess the extent of structural damage and work with you to develop a plan that includes both termite elimination and recommendations for necessary repairs.

Difficulty Opening Windows or Doors

As termites consume wooden door and window frames, they can cause them to warp and become difficult to open or close. This is often an indicator of an infestation within those specific structures. The damage caused by termites to window and door frames can be both cosmetic and functional, affecting the ease of use and potentially leading to drafts or moisture issues. If you notice your windows or doors are sticking or are unusually difficult to operate, and you suspect termite activity, it's important to have it investigated. Our technicians can inspect window sills, door frames, and surrounding wood for signs of termite activity and provide targeted treatment. Identifying these localized infestations early can prevent the spread of termites to other areas of your home.

If you notice any of these signs, it’s crucial to contact a professional termite control service like Jersey City Termite immediately. Early detection and intervention are key to preventing extensive damage and minimizing the cost of repairs. Don't wait – protect your home today.
